A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1957-2015

Missile Man of India

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam's history is one of a distinguished scientist and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he worked for over 40 years as a scientist at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), playing a key role in India's missile and nuclear programs. He was instrumental in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle, SLV-III, and earned the title "Missile Man of India" for his work on ballistic missile technology and the Pokhran-II nuclear tests. He was known as the "People's President" for his popularity and focus on education after his presidency.

Early career: Joined the DRDO in 1958 and later moved to ISRO.

Project director: Served as the project director for India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).

1- Missile programs: Played a vital role in the development of India's missile programs, including the AgniandPrithvi missiles.

2- Nuclear tests: Was a key figure in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.

3- Chief Scientific Adviser: Served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister in the 1990s.

4- President of India: Elected as the 11th President of India in 2002.

5- Post-presidency: Focused on teaching, writing, and public service after his term as president.
